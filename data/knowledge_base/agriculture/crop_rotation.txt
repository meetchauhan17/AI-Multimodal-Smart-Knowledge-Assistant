Domain: Agriculture
Document: Crop Rotation Guidelines
Subject: Sustainable Soil Management

Benefits of Crop Rotation:
- Breaks pest and disease cycles by changing the host plant environment.
- Optimizes soil nutrient utilization (e.g., planting legumes to fix nitrogen).
- Improves soil structure and water retention.

Example 4-Year Rotation Cycle:
- Year 1: Nitrogen Fixers (Legumes: Peas, Beans)
- Year 2: Heavy Feeders (Leafy greens, Brassicas: Cabbage, Broccoli)
- Year 3: Light Feeders (Root vegetables: Carrots, Potatoes)
- Year 4: Grains or Cover Crops (Oats, Rye) to suppress weeds and rest soil.
