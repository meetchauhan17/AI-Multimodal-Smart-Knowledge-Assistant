Exhibit Overview:
Focuses on the Late Cretaceous period (approx. 66-100 million years ago) in North America. Features full-scale skeleton reproductions, fossilized footprints, and interactive displays.

Key Highlights:
- The Tyrannosaurus Rex: A 12-meter-long cast of a sub-adult specimen discovered in South Dakota.
- Triceratops Horridus: Complete skull fossil featuring the iconic three horns and large bony frill.
- Fossil Dig Pit: An interactive activity area for young visitors to excavate simulated dinosaur bones.
